Our Services & Information

Benefits of Debt Counselling

Interest Rates Are Frozen

Your creditors are no longer allowed to charge you additional interest or fees on your existing debts. This is a significant benefit of debt counselling.

Save On Interest

We implement debt review ethically. With an acceptable repayment plan, we get reduced rates for you to save on interest charged by banks.

Payments Are Restructured

Your monthly debt repayments are restructured into one affordable payment based on your current financial situation, ensuring you can meet living expenses.

Avoid More Debt

Because repayments are adjusted to your budget, you won’t have to rely on more credit to cover shortfalls. This prevents the risk of your debt growing.

Legal Protection

Once under debt counselling, creditors cannot take legal action against you or repossess your assets. This protects you from a worse financial situation.

Become Debt-Free

As long as you adhere to the payment plan, your debt will not grow, and you can focus on working towards becoming debt-free without creditor harassment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my information be sent to the credit bureaus?

When you apply for debt counselling, the process involves notifying both credit bureaus and your credit providers. This ensures your credit profile reflects your status as a consumer undergoing debt counselling. While this may temporarily affect your ability to obtain new credit, it also prevents creditors from taking legal action against you.

Can I apply for more credit while under debt review?

No, while you're under debt review, you cannot apply for more credit. This is a condition of the National Credit Act for your own protection, helping you focus on paying off existing debt.

What if my credit providers contact me?

Remain calm and explain that you have applied for debt review. Provide them with your debt counsellor's contact details so they can get in touch directly.

Can I still rent a property?

Yes, you can still rent property. Debt review is not a credit agreement. Your debt counsellor can provide your landlord with proof that you are being financially responsible.

Do debt review clients go to court?

In most cases, you will not have to appear in court. Your case appears before the Magistrates Court or the National Consumers Tribunal, but most clients don't need to go to court personally.

Why Must Fees Be Paid?

Debt review fees are paid to debt counsellors for their professional services. These fees are essential and regulated by the National Credit Regulator (NCR). Debt counsellors are qualified professionals who guide you through the process and negotiate with creditors on your behalf.

Fee Structure:

  • Restructuring Fee: Once-off fee of 100% of your debt rehabilitation amount (up to NCR maximum)
  • Application Fee: Once-off application processing fee
  • Administration Fee: Once-off administration fee as per NCR guidelines
  • Sundry Fee: Legal application fee to NCT or Magistrate Court
  • Monthly Aftercare Fee: Monthly fee for ongoing debt rehabilitation support
  • Payment Distribution Fee (PDA): Monthly transactional fee as per NCR
